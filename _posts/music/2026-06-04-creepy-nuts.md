Surprisingly I haven't done much Japanese stuff yet. I mentioned Gen Hoshino, who I've actually listened to a bit more since that post. Also ginger root but that doesn't really count, he's American, despite some of his songs having Japanese versions, and music videos having a fair amount of Japanese language in the story bits.

Creepy nuts is an interesting one. Those who have watched Dan da dan will be familiar with [Otonoke](https://youtu.be/Pz04moVKyHY?si=Fy_2AV7j_4SI35hb). They seem to really like the 2-3 rhythmic pattern in the bass. I don't know if it has a special name, but it pops up in quite a few of their songs. Fortunately I really like it too, so I tend to enjoy their music. It's probably a bit too 'cool' for most people though. I lack a better descriptor.

If you like Otonoke, check out Nidone and bling blang boom or whatever it's called. I can't be bothered to post links, but if you're already on otonoke the others should just show up.

Next I might do something dedicated to anime music, because it's been where I've found a lot of my Japanese stuff, and it's mostly just one off songs. Some of it's pretty decent though. I found Creepy nuts destinctive enough to merit their own post.
