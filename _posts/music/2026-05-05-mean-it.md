Bear with me a sec.

You know how all music is like rocks, and they all have different shapes and sizes and texture? Ok good, so basically, most music takes rocks and sands them down to make them all nice and smooth and pleasant. And in the most part that's probably a good thing. Maybe some musicians stop sanding too early and are left with a rock that doesn't look quite right. Maybe another musician sanded their rock too much and now it's just a nondescript pebble that sounds exactly the same as all the other boring 4 chord pebbles. And the great songs are the ones that starter off with a nice looking rock and gently guided it to a refined, tastful end product.

[Mean it](https://youtu.be/ly3EJbzRPjc?si=FIhdlUd3uQdvwPeX) feels like taking a big hard lump of granite, and using hammer and chisel to make it as rough as possible. It's very in your face, but musical in it's own way. I also like the message that goes along with it. The video is fun too.
