So this is one will spin out a bit, but I'll start off with the link: [Reelin' in the years](https://youtu.be/AqMqQ6PYYfw?si=Y2OWME8uLpIyzB6O). I've posted the live version, I think it's a bit crisper, slightly faster too.

So the track is originally from Steely Dan, who you should definitely check out, even if as a music history lesson. 

There's quite a lot to unpack here, so this one might be a bit longer. 

The Fearless Flyers, they do instrumental funk. I think it's some of the best instrumental music out there, again with really good musicianship. It's the sort of thing you could put on in the background at parties and suchlike, but actually interesting. Nate Smith is widely considered one of the best drummers, there's a fun video of him doing [7/4 time](https://youtu.be/mDqMRydUNos?si=WVNW7UyyRlmeyq5P), try and clap along. There's also a few guys from Vulfpeck and someone from Snarky Puppy, but Nate runs the show.

I'll do separate posts about Vulfpeck and Cory Wong, as they're 2 of my most listened to artists.
