In my brain there's printed the lyrics "football crazy, football mad", from early childhood. To be honest I'm not sure I know what the reference is, I just know those words. Must have been an early 2000s thing that you just pick up from the atmosphere. Anyway, I played some football today.

Last few days have been holidays, it's golden week here in Japan. Google it if you need, but basically it's loads of bank holidays smashed together and everyone goes travelling.

Apparently not as many people are travelling during golden week these days, partly due to cost of living, the Japanese economy isn't in great shape at the moment. 

I've been doing a bit of shopping, bought a notebook to force me to finally learn some kanji. I'd like to think I can recognise quite a lot, but haven't done any real writing practice, which makes me fuzzy with the details, so sometimes I'll misidentify characters. Apparently writing is just really good for burning things into the brain too, so why not. Because I'm lazy that's why.

Planning to go out a few times to various spots in Tokyo, but honestly not planned anything grand. It's been nice catching up on a few jobs too. But I'll try and get out to Akihabara, Koenji and Shibuya in the next days.

My sleep has been a bit off of late, I go to bed early but take a while to sleep, and sometimes my brain will be racing with thoughts which usually doesn't happen. Maybe it's understimulated, maybe over. I don't really know. Either way, I end up with loads of thoughts about different things I could be doing, like what Japanese to study and how to study it, social events to go to or not go to, things I need to buy, eat, games to play etc.

As a result my morning exercise has taken a bit of a hit, not been going quite as often, and when I do go I have a bit less energy. Pullups and dips at an outdoor gym are the main things I do for upper, although I can really only do a few of each. I'll do mechanical drop sets (I think that's what they're called) to get a few more reps in, but I probably need to dial in my routines a bit better over the coming days and weeks.

I don't think it's too bad a thing though, having the mind racing like this at times. I think a few months ago, before I came here, my brain was always playing catchup at the end of the day, it didn't have much bandwidth for that kind of activity. It would just be like "I'm too tired, go to sleep". If I did have a racing mind it would also probably be a lot of negative energy and anxiety instead of ideas and inspirations. So I'm not too worried, I'll try and reflect on what my brain is trying to tell me.

Recently I've been really enjoying Tokyo. The weather has been just right for the last month. Not too hot, not too cold. Ignoring the couple of rainy days which were honestly just a bit too much. Chill out, Tokyo, we get it. Next time it rains I'm bringing an extra pair of socks to class...

It's been nice settling into a rhythm of school, lunch, homework, shopping, cooking cleaning eating. Nothing feels too rushed or stressful. Sometimes I'll get a melonpan or cake as an evening treat, or find a random ramen packet to try, just to see what's out there. Sometimes I'll go to the gym with a friend, but I'm not relentlessly pursuing any fitness goals at the moment so it's not too much of a headache. In the mornings I can pop to the park and do some exercises and try and fail to chat with the group of old people that share coffee where I exercise. I'm sliding away from having to 'complete' Tokyo, towards just living here and existing happily.

Of course, it's not long term sustainable, I'm not making any money right now. But I also don't feel like I'd be too dismayed about returning home when the time comes. There are things I miss for sure. There will also definitely be things I miss about Tokyo when I do leave. Abundance of fried chicken for a start. People commenting in amazement at how tall I am, as if I were a mythical being sent down by the gods.

So yeah, as I was saying, I played football today. It was fun, my toes really hurt I think they got friction burned because they're all soft and not used to changes of direction any more. I scored a few goals, including a nice far post run from a corner, and a outside foot poke across goal after some crisp build up. Back held up just about. I'll probably try and go again at some point.

Been enjoying the basketball still, round 1 is wrapping up. Was nice seeing the 76ers beat the Celtics. Looking forward to round 2. East looks competetive, west looks like it'll be between Thunder and Spurs.

I do still have a to-do list, despite what I was saying earlier. I'm looking for some jeans. Haven't worn jeans since, like, mid high school? Tried some on in uniqlo and thought they looked pretty good surprisingly, but size was too big. Tried the size smaller the other day but they just fit wrong. Apparently Japan is pretty good for denim so I'll see what I can find. Might as well take advantage of the strong pound. I bought some linen trousers that I think are pretty nice. But I don't know much about trousers to be honest. 

The other, bigger thing is planning my summer. To be honest I've enjoyed Tokyo so much I'm thinking of staying longer. I don't remember if I talked about this last time, I probably did, but maybe a sharehouse or something to see what that's like. I'm also concious of the increasingly heavy suitcase I'd be lugging around if I did travel, so I'd probably want to put it in long term storage if I did decide to travel, but that's another chunk of admin to do. Maybe if I got a cheapish sharehouse I could just go on holidays from there, and leave stuff behind. Feels like a very cost inefficient method, but if it's convenient, could be the way to go.

I'm voting by proxy in the coming local elections, fun! Well for me it is, I don't have to do anything. Dad might be a bit put out though...

Lastly, I've started reading manga. It's so hard there's so much I don't understand and it takes forever to get through. Took about 3 days to get through the first story. There are 700 total. I think I just need to keep going and not get discouraged, and over time it'll get easier. It's a good way of improving vocab steadily too. I've recently learned you can just export dictionary definitions to Anki, I've customised the card settings too to generate tts Japanese audio. The dictionary I use also has example phrases for a lot of words, which I find really useful. This way I can hopefully improve vocab and listening comprehension, while practicing my reading. Key thing is now I've got no excuses I just have to do it. In general I'm a bit more motivated with my Japanese learning at the moment.
