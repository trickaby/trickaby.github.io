So last week was golden week, which meant no school Monday to Wednesday. It's made time feel very loose and elastic, and I'm struggling to remember what we did.

Mostly I stayed at home, or maybe went for a walk here or there. That and doing chores. Went to Akihabara with some friends which was fun. I bought loads of pokemon games so now I don't really feel like I need any more. I also started playing ソウルシルバー (sooru sirubaa), which is Japanese for soul silver...

It's a really good game, probably the best remake. But it's taking me ages to get through. Even though I know the story really well, reading in Japanese is really difficult and slow. It doesn't use any Chinese characters, which is easier for kids and novices that can't read any, but once you get used to Kanji it feels easier than without. Luckily they have spaces between words, which makes things a bit easier to parse.

Had a few quiet school days before the weekend, to be honest I don't really remember much of what we studied. It's still fairly easy, but I'm getting a lot of good practice in, and the days of the month are finally starting to sink in, after a good year and a half of neglect.

The weekend was also pretty uneventful. I didn't have a very good Saturday, just never really got going for some reason. Sunday was a bit better, and had football in the evening, which to be honest wasn't the best. Our team was pretty rubbish and we lost every game. I tried hard to not blame myself too much. Football was not really about winning, but when you lose a lot it does make it less fun.

I also made a fun music discovery, which I'll write about, but in the music section. It's not really relevant or interesting.

In general I've been feeling less motivated to travel around. I think it's my default state, wanting to just stay in all the time. There's a healthy balance to be struck for sure, and I'm probably straying away from that balance a bit. Maybe over the weekend or something I'll plan a little day out somewhere.
