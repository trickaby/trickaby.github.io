Already June, which means it's the last month of this term of Japanese school. Soon the friends I've made will be heading home. Some are staying though, and I'll meet new people. It's gone really quick though, feels like I'm still just getting settled in.

Had an enjoyable last week and a half sine last update. Football's been going well. Sometimes I enjoy it more than others, and it seems to be quite strongly correlated to whether or not I win. I tend to just chat a bit to the people I'm on a team with, which seems to work for me. Over time I'll probably get to know people a bit more.

Studies are going pretty well, I feel like I'm working fairly hard at the moment. On the flip side I haven't been out and about much. I did go to DiverCity with school friends Friday afternoon, it was pretty fun. There was a Pokemon Go event going on, so there were some awnings and various pokemon themed sights to see. Was pretty cool but I didn't really know much about what the event was for. Maybe an anniversary? 10 years seems about right for when it came out.

Also saw the big gundam. There's some new photos up from that day. Of the usual style. I got one of my friends to take a few pictures of me with an inflatable pikachu, I think he's a natural.

I'm making slow and steady progress through my pokemon games. I'm alternating between scarlett and ソウルシルバー, depending on how much I can be bothered to practice Japanese. I also spent about 3 hours the other day playing pokemon quiz, where I just have to name as many pokemon as possible. There's just over 1000 total. I got to about 900 before I had to rely on silhouettes. If I'd spent more time on it I'd probably have gotten more, but after a while you get tired.

Oh there was a typhoon today, so class was done remotely. Brought back memories of covid days. It was nice being able to have a bit of a lie in, and having more time in the day to do laundry and cleaning, but I do prefer the classroom overall.

One thing I'm noticing recently is that I'm not great at talking to people for whom English is not their first language. I don't spend much of my life yapping, so when I do, my vocabulary can be a bit awkward. Partly I think it's because I like using slightly unconventional word choices, and part of my sense of humour is playing around with words and misusing grammar. I'll often go out of my way to find the exact right word that describes my thoughts, instead of using simpler words. Also sometimes I'll just be a nuisance and throw in British English phrases that even my American friends won't really understand. I think I tend to talk pretty quiet and mumbly too which doesn't help.

I have gone some way to deciding how far I want to take my language study. A lot of metrics go by the Japanese language proficiency test, JLPT. The grades are N1-5, with N1 being the highest level of proficiency. N2 is usually what's needed to get a job in Japan. I've decided that my measure of success is that I am able to be funny in Japanese. Honestly I think it's a pretty ambitious goal. I'd like to think I'm pretty funny in English, and hopefully it transfers over to Japanese once I get good enough. Every time I try to make a joke in Japanese class though, sensei just has to correct my grammar instead of saying I'm funny. I'll get there some day I guess. Sometimes I'll make my Vietnamese classmates laugh though when we do pair practice, and they don't speak a lick of English. So it must be possible.

Also, apologies I've been meaning to do one of these a week, Sunday appears to have been the designated day but sometimes I get tired after football and forget. I think it's also in my best interests because otherwise I'll forget what I've been up to.

I also want to do a few more music posts. Again, mostly for my own benefit.
