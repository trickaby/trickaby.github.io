Apologies haven't written anything in a while.
Since my last post, which marked the end of the spring travels, I've been getting settled in to day-to-day life in Tokyo.

Started off by getting sorted with basic supplies, shampoo, sponges, soap etc. It's actually pretty hard finding everything, especially for me where I agonise over every decision. Also because I can't read much of what's in the shops. I did resort to getting a few things from Japanese amazon when I got too confused or lost. Also started scouting out places to exercise.

School started too, on Monday. I was pretty stressed out about it all, in particular with some of the admin. I had to get my confirmation of address from city hall and that took up most of the first week getting sorted. I think having it in the back of my mind the whole time was a bit unhelpful.

Also I get pretty shy with a bunch of strangers, I suppose everyone does a bit but I don't have that skill of being able to go up to someone and say hello. Luckily other people in the class do, so I got to know some people anyway.

A pattern is starting to be established of going for lunch with a small group of classmates, as our class runs from 9:10-12:40. By the end of class we're all starving and exhausted. Food has been good so far, we've had tonkatsu, gyudon, Chinese, and tempura. All very tasty. If I had to pick a favourite, probably tempura. I also learned a new method of ordering, which is to buy your food outside on a machine, then give your ticket to the staff. 

One thing I like about Japan is that you, by default, either go up to the till to pay, or pay before you eat. One thing that annoys me about some restaurants is when you finish and you spend 20 minutes trying to find a waiter to ask for the bill, then you have to wait for them to go and give you the bill, then wait a bit longer for them to come back with the card reader. This way, you can pretty much just get up and go when you're ready.

School itself has been decent. We're starting again from scratch, which is fine but currently we're not learning much of anything new. Most people in my class are vaguely around the same level as me, obviously with a lot of variance though. The style of teaching is a lot of repetition and review, which at the moment is tedious at times, but I'm sure once we start learning new stuff it will start feeling very fast paced and overwhelming. As long as I keep up with homeworks and vocabs I should be ok though. I'm still getting a bit bamboozled by informal verb endings though.

Food wise, I tend to do a small shop pretty much every day. Usually it's like a quarter of a regular shop, where I've run out of 1 or 2 basics like milk or tofu, then end up getting a couple of other things like treat or sauces or something. Hopefully as I build up my pantry and get better at stocking up on things, I won't need to shop as often. It is quite nice though just to pop out in the evening for a bit of a wander though. The shops aren't far at all.

I've been eating a lot of sweet things like pastries or biscuits in the evenings. Outside of that though I'm fairly healthy so it hasn't slowed me down too much so far. 

Starting up with exercise has been slow progress to be honest. Weekdays it's harder to fit it in, it's usually 3 or 4 by the time I get home, and I'm usually in need of a break by then. I've also got to do homework and cook, as well as doing odd jobs like the shop or laundry.

To be clear I do have enough time for exercise, I just haven't gotten into a routine yet, which is the tricky bit. I did go to the park yesterday (Saturday 11th) to do pullups, but the whole area was taken up by a kids baseball group. I went out for a walk later and happened to end up back there, where there was space for me to do pullups so I ended up getting it done. The park is on my way to school, so maybe I could start doing it either before or after class.

I also found another place by the river today. It's actually a bit closer than the other park, but in the opposite direction from school. But it's got rings for pullups, and they're a bit higher off the ground. One issue I've had is that most bars are a bit too low, so I have to bend my legs a bit then my hamstrings start cramping. Probably partly because I'm not training legs enough but it's still annoying.

The place by the river is also a potential running spot, seems less crowded too. Still not sure if I want to take up running, I never really enjoy it, but maybe I just need to stick with it for a bit.

In terms of weekday exercise, probably 2 weekdays and 2 weekend days is enough for exercise. I could potentially wake up 6, go park from 6:30-7:30, have a quick breakfast and wash, then head to class at 8:30 ready for 9:10 start. But when I'm against the clock it makes me anxious, then exercise is less effective. An alternative would be to go at some point in the evening when I'm a bit more tired, but I could rest at home for a bit before heading out. Anyway, I've got options.

There is a gym about 5 minutes away, which is very tempting. But for some reason I don't fancy going to a gym. It feels like this is a good opportunity to do some other forms of exercise in other places.

One of the parks also has an astro turf, where I see people playing football or baseball. It's made me want to join some pickup football matches somewhere. I'd have to buy some shoes and get into slightly better shape, but maybe I can join something local.

It's taking a while for me to find out what things I actually like doing for fun. Obviously a lot of people point to the nightlife and shopping in Tokyo, but neither of those are really my thing. As much as I want to experience what Tokyo has to offer, there are some parts that just aren't for me, so there's not much point in me taking an hours trudge to some corner of Tokyo to see a cool shopping centre because I don't really do shopping for fun much.

What I have enjoyed has been just wandering around, finding parks, conbini, and enjoying the cherry blossoms. I've done a few weekend walks and they've always been really enjoyable, so maybe an idea is to find more of those. Someone at class also mentioned some game places, which sound fun. I'm not sure if it's like an arcade or mini golf type stuff, either way I'd probably enjoy that sort of thing as a social activity. The language school is also planning a trip to Shinjuku park on Friday, which I'm looking forward to. I've already been twice but it'll be fun anyway.

I tend to eat at home when I'm by myself, I don't enjoy eating out alone as much. I've made some nice things so far though, katsu curry being the highlight. Initially I struggled with buying seasoning, especially because I'm not here long enough to buy loads of different things. I've settled on buying seasoning mixes and sauces, instead of individual ingredients. The exceptions being soy, wasabi, garlic and ginger pastes, and sesame oil. They feel more essential. So for the katsu for example, I just bought a sauce packet you microwave, then cooked my own rice and veg and chicken and sweet potato in the pan. Turns out the sauce packet also had bits of chicken and things inside it so I didn't need to, but oh well. Mistakes will continue until Japanese reading proficiency improves.
