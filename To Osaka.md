Took a couple of trains from Kyoto to Osaka, interestingly we didn't need to go into Kyoto station and back out, we could just take a train towards Nara, then another one into Osaka. Took an hour and a half, which is slower than the bullet train would have been, but this was easier as we just had to tap in and out with an IC card. I still need to get to grips with the different train fares, because some of them need an extra ticket.

Arrived in Namba, put our suitcases in a coin locker, then headed out to explore. Namba has a lot of food and shopping, so it was fun to walk around and see what they have. In Dotonbori, we ate some takoyaki, karage, wagyu skewers, omelette. All were very tasty, but there were so many stalls it was a bit overwhelming and tough to decide what to get. What we did get was very nice though.

Walked around the busy streets, saw the running man that's very famous but to be honest I still don't really know why. 

In Kyoto we pretty much avoided the really busy city centre parts, apart from Nishiki market. We both prefer quieter parts, it's pretty draining navigating the crowds and I get a bit of sensory overload.

Got a bit lost in Namba station trying to find our stored luggage, but found it in the end. Train back to our Airbnb, which gave us the wrong address initially, so had to sit in a park and figure it out. Arrived in the early evening and chilled out for the rest of the day.

Our place is pretty interesting, it's a house with a couple of bedrooms, and very low doorways. I've got to be careful not to hit my head pretty much everywhere, but it's a nice home and feels like we're living like locals.
