Day 2 was similar in structure to day 1, but a bit less intense, with better weather and eating more food.

Started off dragging mum out of bed for a 7am start, which ended up being 7:15. Took a train to Arashiyama, which is where the bamboo forest is. Walked around, ended up avoiding the crowded bits, walked along the river for a bit and looked at ducks. Walked up and down some hills and stuff, before heading back to the station after a couple of hours.

Bought some emergency rations from a family mart, fruit and drinks and sandwiches etc.

Headed to the golden temple, Kinkaku-ji. It was really nice if a bit crowded with tourists, there was pretty much one path to stick to so not much space to explore. The temple itself was really cool though and gave some good photo opportunities. At the end there was an overpriced honey and ginger flavour ice cream, which was actually really nice. The cone was matcha flavour which was good too. I didn't take a picture of it but it was worthy of one.

I famously have only one dietary restriction which is that I don't eat ice cream. This rule is flexible for times when I fancy ice cream, such as this day. 

After temple was a temple. This time Daitoku-ji. It was a lot quieter, and actually really peaceful and well done. We both really enjoyed it and I'm not sure why more people weren't there. Had a little tea experience too, where they serve tea that is from a pot on coals, then whisk it with their little bamboo whisk that you always see being used. It came with a sweet which was also very nice, no clue what it was though. They had a bit of zen garden stuff too, which was good. But the main thing was just how peaceful and quiet it was, and how you left feeling lighter and more refreshed than when you entered. I bought a book on zen meditation which came in English and Japanese, so I look forward to reading that. Maybe in a few years time I'll try and revisit it in Japanese.

After temple we went for lunch - ramen! Finally! It was really nice, just a random high street ramen-ya that the locals would frequent. We got soy ramen with a bit of fried chicken and rice on the side. The noodles were really good, as well as the broth. Sasuga. It also came with a chill/garlic sauce on the table, which gave a nice burst of flavour too. Although we ate a lot it didn't fell heavy.

Headed back home after that, wanting to be careful not to exhaust ourselves too much too early. still a decent amount of walking though, 9 miles today and 13 yesterday.
