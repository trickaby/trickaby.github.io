So I've been a listener of Vulf for a few years, friends and family will be sick of me talking about them. But that's their problem.

This popped up recently, a new version of Jackie Evans' original. I'd never heard of her, but she has my attention now. I'm always impressed by someone who can play the piano and sing at the same time. But besides that, she sings well and the song has a real groove, especially the vulf version.

If you're interested, here's the original, [non-vulf version](https://youtu.be/KTEe2jKsl_U?si=EFti76e2hvzpEP6a). 

Initially I really preferred the vulf version, it was a lot heavier and more punchy. But recently I've been leaning more to the original, it has a fun, bouncy quality. Maybe it's a bit busy in parts but I kind of like that, it feels youthful. I'm not sure what you will think, maybe the songs just sound the same to most people. A bit like "I'm coming out" by Dianna Ross. There's the original mix that everyone knows, but apparently there was another mix by Nile Rodgers that was more 'chic' sounding, less motown. [See here](https://youtu.be/WZviVJfepDM?si=8JdblfPy1oGMWZSr) if you're interested. I think it's fascinating but I suspect most people won't.

It reminds me of a Van Gogh I saw at the Rijksmuseum that was from his earlier years. It was a lot more bold and dynamic than his more famous stuff, but I really liked it for that exact reason. There were certain imperfections and imbalances that I was drawn to. Anyway that's a bit of a tangent. Most of these won't be this long.
