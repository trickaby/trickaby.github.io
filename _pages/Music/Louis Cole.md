So I had to do a Louis Cole post, I've been wearing his hat all around Japan for the last few months.

Listening advice: pay attention more to the musical arrangement than the singing, it's way more interesting. Drums, bass, then how harmony is layered on top. The vocals are just part of that rich instrumentation most of the time. That and a vessel for strange (but interesting) lyrics.

What is Louis Cole's music like? It's a bit weird, in my opinion the correct amount. But there's so much musicality hidden behind a goofy, unserious facade. He's a really good drummer first off, you could listen to him just for the drums. He also is sneaky amazing at writing harmony, especially for strings. He's also just cool, I've seen someone described him as Jacob Collier but cool, which I think is pretty accurate. I think Collier is a genius, I just think his music is a bit hit or miss.

Ya like syncopation? [Here you go](https://youtu.be/39vouYxa55M?si=XOxKtEPqKpUzbME3). This is probably my favourite song of his. Has a bit of everything. I'd pay attention to the kit part, it's so complex I still haven't really gotten my head around it. This [Knower track](https://youtu.be/NDpeHQUSWT0?si=G1vvFjIhzb9-GsBQ) is similar.

I listen to this when I'm feeling sad: [Things](https://youtu.be/RhllQAiEQlM?si=uE556_UV4BPK_Vx0). Then I listen to this straight after: [Let it happen](https://youtu.be/fkQgAN9xRO4?si=WUzhseuAeWUfXhXA). Then youtube will generally recommend [Night](https://youtu.be/NR6RtSqrcgc?si=CMt0RD56qui22CD0).

All of these have a similar sense of melancholy, but to me they're not depressing. They give more of a feeling of helplessness, that we're all just hurtling through space doing arbitrary nonsense, but that's ok. That's probably as well as I can describe it, it's a feeling more than anything. Most music feels like it's going into my brain, this stuff feels like something is leaving.

Anyway, here's [window shop](https://youtu.be/_VC0oM-fdeE?si=sd0PS4gRdxThCjac). I'm just fascinated by the texture of the drums. They sound so coarse. It's as if he went on a mission to make drums sound as 'bad' as possible, but in a way that sounds cool? From watching a few interviews and podcasts and stuff, I've learned that this stuff is always intentional with him, he's going through painstaking efforts to make it sound just like this. The lyrics are sneaky good too, as with a lot of his stuff.

The funny thing is, I didn't actually like Louis Cole the first few times I listened to him.

Bonus:

[Thundercat - I love Louis Cole](https://youtu.be/YHTufn3VWq0?si=Y0bfef3Bl7xCoxjH). Again, the drums and bass carry this. One of my favourite songs in existence.
