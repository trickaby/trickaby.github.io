This was the big do-loads-of-things day in Hiroshima. We'd booked a tour of the atomic bomb museum and Miyajima.

The first half of the day was going around the Peace Memorial. There was the bomb dome, as well as a few memorial things. All of it really interesting, and it helped having a guide to explain the significance of some of the statues. Next was the museum which was very intense, but glad we went. I didn't have a good grasp of exactly what happened when the bomb fell, so it was a good educational experience for me. A really sad thing for me was how much the people suffered when they died.

I was also surprised at how quickly the city recovered. It was safe again a year after the bomb dropped apparently, for a few reasons.
- The bomb exploded in the air above the city, which is better than in the ground for radiation
- It wasn't as powerful as one might think, being the first of its kind

We then headed to Miyajima Island. We had okonomiyaki for lunch. It's different to Osakan okonomiyaki, and apparently there's quite a big debate over which one is the real version, and which is better.

I liked them both, but not wanting to be a fence sitter, I'll go with the Hiroshima version, slightly. It was nice having the noodles, and having the cabbage just plain and unprocessed.

Then we went to Itsukushima Shrine. I'm a bit ashamed to say I only here learned the difference between a temple and a shrine. I guess I'd never thought to question it. Basically, shrine = Shinto, temple = Buddhism. There, nice and easy.

This is where the famous big tori gate is. It isn't buried deep in the ground, it's just really heavy which is how it stands up. Got some nice pictures courtesy of the tour guide, who was well practised with this particular skill.

We continued to the Daishoin temple. There were a lot of small statues wearing hats. There was also a dark passage, which was a little staircase under a room thing, which leads to a passage which is, well, dark. You follow the wall to your left, not being able to see a thing, until a few lit images to your left appear. You carry on for a bit until you reach the exit. It's not very long, but walking in the dark is very unnerving so it feels long. The idea is you lose yourself in the darkness, and are reborn on the other side. Sounds very Buddhist to me.

After that we walked back towards the ferry. We had time to try a few steamed oysters and lemon soda, which were recommended to us. Both very nice. If we had more time or appetite, it would have been nice to try grilled oyster, but that's ok.

That was the end of our tour, so we headed back to Hiroshima station. From there, I wanted to check out Arashiyama park. It was cool but we didn't get there until sunset, so couldn't see much. I also was aware we'd been out and about a lot so didn't want to tire us out even more. We got some cool sunset pictures though. Then headed back home via 711 to get me an egg sandwich and some nuts.

As we went to the kitchen (shared) for some milk and cookies (biscuits), there was a caligraphy class about to finish. They asked if we wanted to try, so we thought why not. See the [Photos](/spring_travels/Hiroshima) for why not.

It was good fun though, and we got a little souvenir out of it.

Shower, bed, onto tomorrow.
