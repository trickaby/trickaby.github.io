title: To Hiroshima
---

From Onomichi to Hiroshima, you can take a bullet train but instead we opted for the slower but cheaper local trains. I quite like the local trains to be honest, they're pretty relaxed. It wasn't really that much longer anyway, and we still had time to walk around in Hiroshima before check-in and grab lunch.

There was a bit of a walk from the station to our room, but once we got there we could dump our luggage. After that we headed to a local tsukemen restaurant. Tsukemen is like ramen but the noodles are served cold, and there's a dipping broth/sauce served alongside. The noodle plate was also topped with onion, spring onion, cabbage, and chicken slices. We also got a beef bone broth ramen to share. Both were very tasty and mum got a lesson in how to eat it properly. We were given some spicy miso on the side, which was really good. Mum tried to dump it all in, but the guy came and said no, put it into this small bowl and take a bit of broth and mix. Otherwise you've made the whole bowl too spicy.

The food was really good, probably the best ramen I've had here so far. The tsukemen was also a really nice new thing to try. I would get it again for sure.

We wandered around a bit before checking in. Hiroshima seems a bit more of a working town, less touristy than I imagined. It's also pretty modern looking, which in some ways isn't so surprising.

We had a bit of bento for dinner before a fairly early night.
