From Hokkaido, took a taxi to the train station then a train to the plane station then a plane to another plane station. 
Landed in Osaka and picked up mum who landed a few hours later. Got sorted with train ticket and IC card which is like a Japanese oyster.
Bit of a treck to get to our accoms in Kyoto, had to take an hours train from the airport, then 45 minutes walk to the hotel room. We were both tired from travels but excited to start our adventure.

Went to the restaurant opposite across the road, which was a Nepalese curry restaurant. I know we should be eating Japanese food but I was interested to see what the Japanese version of a curry would be like. We got the special salad, and a chicken curry with rice and naan to be as standard as possible. It was pretty nice but the curry and rice portions were the inverse of what I had in mind - not much curry and loads of rice. It was decent though, not very spicy at all which I've heard is common. The guy did give us some chilli powder to make it hotter, but by then I was nearly finished so didn't try it. Overall it was good and pretty cheap, we were too tired to care much about what we ate.
