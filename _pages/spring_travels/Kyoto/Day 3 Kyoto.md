Last day proper in Kyoto, went to Nanzen-ji, another temple. It was similarly quiet to Daitoku-ji, but a bit less so, with more in it like painted walls and a short film about the history of the temple. Then we walked along the philosopher's path, which is a walk along a small stream. We then headed to Shugakuin Imperial Villa, which is in the northeastern corner of Kyoto, up in the hills.

The villa has to be booked in advance to guarantee a place, as they only run guided tours 4 times a day. We didn't book but tried our luck at the door. We arrived at 11:30, and the next tour wasn't until 1:30. We booked our slot then went for lunch, soba noodles, which were tasty. Similar to the previous day, seemed like a fairly local spot for people to grab lunch on the cheap and cheerful.

After lunch we went back for our tour. The grounds were really nice and well maintained. The sun had come out too which makes everything look nicer. We were taken around some of the houses, before finishing with a walk around a lake. All of it very pleasant and it made for good photos.

We stopped by a bakery to pick up sandwiches for the evening, before making our way home for the evening.
