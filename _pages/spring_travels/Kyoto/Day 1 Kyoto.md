Day 1 in Kyoto was full of walking, we went to:
- Fushimi Inari Taisha
- Ninenzaka
- Maruyama park
- Yasaka Shrine
- Pontocho alley
- Nishiki market

Basically loads of temples then some market streets. Had some gyoza, tempura and fried fish in Nishiki. Then we were tired so went home, walked about 13 miles total. Best part was probably the first place, Fushimi Inari Taisha. Went nice and early and it was pouring with rain so it wasn't too busy, it's basically loads of shrines and torii gates winding up a hill. We also found a little side path to take us away that was completely quiet. Was nice to get away from the city into the peace and quiet, we were surprised how easily we were able to escape the crowds.

As we headed back into the city later in the day, we did find the more busy spots, seems like Kyoto has a decent balance of energy and peace. I tend to favour the quieter parts but I can appreciate some liveliness can be good too. Pontocho alley was nice but they say it's better to visit as an evening dinner thing. Nishiki market was cool as well, I got a bit overwhelmed with all the choices and probably should have done a bit of research into what to look out for, but that's ok.

Been sat around for the last few weeks so the long day of walking has been tiring. It's good though, we got a lot done so hopefully the next couple of days will be at a more relaxed pace. We're expecting this day to be the worst in terms of weather too, so not having to trudge around in rain gear will help too.

One thing we need to figure out is our eating pattern. I'm sort of torn between saving room for eating out and trying food, and just getting stuff that's healthy and gives me energy. I'm missing my big Hokkaido breakfast buffet a bit, cos that let me just load up on fruit and veggies.
