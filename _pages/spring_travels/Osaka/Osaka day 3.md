Day 3 in Osaka was castle day. We headed to Osaka park, which is a huge park space, with a lot of smaller sections of various flavours. My favourite was the plum grove which was just starting to blossom. We didn't go inside the castle itself, were happy to just walk around the grounds and see some nice views. There was a museum next to the castle which was surprisngly informative, given it was essentially just a room with a bunch of souvenir shops and restaurants surrounding it. The castle has been around for nearly 1500 years as a walled city, then eventually turned into a castle. It's been burned down, struct by lightning in it's history, and it's current iteration has been around for about 100 years as it was rebuilt thanks to donations from the public.

For dinner we went to a local okinomiyaki restaurant, which was a bit of a treat. We had octopus okonomiyaki and a set meal teppanaki, which was topped with a tower of steak, prawns, squid, sauce and mayo. Very tasty but also very filling. After that we went back home.
