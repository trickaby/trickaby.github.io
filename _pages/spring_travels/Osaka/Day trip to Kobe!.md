For our last full day in Osaka, we went to not Osaka. Kobe is only an hour or so away by train, so we thought we might as well head over. It takes less time than it does for me to get into work...

Kobe was a really nice day out. We went to Merikan park, which is a bit of open space by the sea. There was a small information point, almost like a mini outdoor museum, detailing the 1995 earthquake, and rebuilding the pier in Kobe. The whole thing took about 2 and a half years, and is pretty impressive. Not that I know much about pier building.

We continued along the seafront, walking through a few shopping places and through a ferry terminal. It was a Monday morning so pretty quiet. It's still early spring and the weather is sunny but cool. Probably won't start to get busy for another few weeks I reckon.

Kobe is famous for its beef. We didn't try any because we had a teppanyaki the day before which had loads of beef and prawns and squid on it.

We walked along Motomachi shopping street, a long covered street with all sorts of shops. It was ok but didn't feel super exciting. Probably better if you actually had something you wanted to buy.

There is also the largest Chinatown in Japan in Kobe, so we went there and had a few steamed buns at one of the many stalls. They were alright but by then I was a bit tired from walking around and not much in the mood for food.

Next we went to Sorakuen park, which is a Japanese garden. It was really nice, the classic manicured trees and bushes, stone paths and koi ponds. All really nice. Was a good relaxing way to wind down after lots of walking around the seafront and city streets.

After Sorakuen park, we headed back to Osaka. Feeling like we hadn't seen enough of the city, we got off at Osaka station to walk around. Just followed our noses for a bit until we'd had enough, again going through a lot of shopping streets and street food places. Then headed back home to rest for the evening.

We haven't really gone out much in the evenings, usually because we're too tired by the end of the day. I think that's fine though, the things we're interested in tend to be things we can do during the day.

Got some sushi from the supermarket to have at home, then turned in.
