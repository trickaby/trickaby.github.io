As promised, we were given a lift to Shin-Yokohama, which was a bit further away than the local Tokiwa station, but it saved us having to take a slow and infrequent local train from Tokiwa to Shin-Yokohama.

We got the 11:11 to Tokyo, which was a roughly 4 hour journey to get us into Yokohama. On the way we spotted Fuji from the train window, it was pretty amazing even from our poor viewpoint. Train journey passed by without any major event, and we checked into our accommodation in Chinatown. 

Our room is pretty funny, it's super narrow and we both have to sleep on a long bunkbed, underneath which is the living area. I'm having to do a lot of minding my head as usual. Funnily enough the bathroom is as wide as the rest of the room so feels pretty spacious.

After catching up on the day's sumo wrestling, we met up with my friend from Japanese class in the evening, and her friend. We walked aimlessly around Chinatown which is always fun. Popped into a temple which was cool, then wandered around before finding something to eat. I had dan dan noodles, and the table had won ton noodles and shared some dim sum.

After that we walked around the waterfront, which looked pretty nice at night. So far we haven't done much night time walking, usually too tired by then. It was good though, and given we'd spent hours sat on trains we had the energy for it.

We then parted ways and headed back home for the night.
