Started off by having some fruit and bread for breakfast with tea, then took the bus to Sankeien Garden, to the south of Yokohama. Bus was all good except mum nearly crushed a lady when she got jolted by the bus moving.

Garden was nice, you could tell it was just waiting another week or two before it would explode with the spring flowers and blossoms. Was still a really nice place though and plenty to look at, while being nice and quiet too. A few ponds and streams too.

Next we went to the cup noodle museum. Neither of us really knew much about it, which was probably for the best, as if we'd known we probably wouldn't have gone. It was good fun in the end though. We had a cup noodle creation thing, where they allow you to book a ticket, which lets you queue up, which allows you to buy an empty cup, which grants access to a table and pens via another queue. After decorating the cup and marking with the correct date, you may join the main queue to create your cup of noodles, with a choice of seasonings and toppings.

It sounds like a faff but honestly it was pretty good fun, and the queues move pretty quickly so it doesn't feel too laborious. Got an edible souvenir out of it too. It's definitely more for kids but grown ups can have fun too if they try.

After that we wondered back down the harbour, came across a market that turned out to be all for dogs, so we made a sharp turn out and away.

Walked back through Yamashita park, which we'd seen the previous evening at night. It was much better during the day to be honest. You could actually see the flowers. They had lupins which apparently aren't so common any more. Maybe flowers are like fashions where things go in and out. What a fickle world we live in.

Walked back through Chinatown, this time with a street foods strategy. It's worth bearing in mind it's around 3pm by now and we've not eaten anything since breakfast. Picked up a Taiwanese pepper bun, took it home to eat. It's pretty convenient that we're based in Chinatown actually, I'm impressed with my prior self. Usually he books silly places in silly places.

The pepper bun was really good, flaky bready pastry with sesame seeds and a peppery meat filling, with some green onions in there too. I'm pretty sure it was beef but could have been pork. Either way the dominant flavour was pepper and it was good.

After a bit of a sit down and some tea, and after having watched some domestic baseball and sumo wrestling, we ventured back out for more street food. This time *yaki shoronpo*, which is soup dumplings but pan seared to give them a bit more structure. It had a really good dumplingy flavour, and the broth was plentiful and prone to eruption.

Next was that comically large Taiwanese fried chicken, we got one that was a bit smaller, not knowing what size it would be. I think I'm glad it was smaller though, because it's pretty samey after a while. Glad I tried it to know I can revert to 7/11 and famimart fried chicken in the future. I'm not really missing out on anything by doing so.

Then we had something sweet, another sesame bun this time filled with red bean paste. Can't go wrong, tasted exactly like you'd epect. Cruchy ish outside with sesame seeds, inside was soft and squidgy like a mochi. I got brown sugar bubble tea after because I haven't had one in ages and it looked good. It was.

Got back about 6, enough time to wind down, shower etc before tucking into our pot noodle creations:

Mine:
- Cup noodle seasoning
- shrimp
- green onions
- fish cake
- Garlic

Mum's:
- Seafood seasoning
- fish cake
- shrimp
- kimchi
- crab sticks

I think mum's had a more well defined vision, mine was a bit of a garbled mess.

In the end I preferred mine, I think it was mainly just the difference in broth flavour.
