Day started off with toast and fruit. There isn't a toaster here, or grill or oven. So we toasted the bread in a wok, as one does. Got some butter the other day too so it was buttery and nice. The batch of oranges we bought last week have lasted us ages because they're so bitter we're too scared to eat them. Finished them today though.

After breakfast we went to Shinjuku. Again. This time with a bitter determination to finally see the park there. We got in, despite some fairly substantial queues, and got inside. The park itself was really nice. Loads of open space, grassy spaces too which I've found to be a bit rare here. Also rare was the ability to walk/sit on the grassy spaces. I made sure to stay out of the long grass though, as I didn't bring any Poké balls.

This was the first day of proper full throated cherry blossom. Loads of it all over the place, fully open. Got some nice pictures but honestly after a bit the photos lose a bit of meaning. It's better to see in person to take in the volume, as well as the constrast between the different varieties. Someone had also done a painting on an easel in the cherry grove. It was rubbish. 

There was also a Japanese style garden on one side, with the usual suspects of pond, carp, bridges, sculpted pine trees, gravel paths. Really nice though, and on a slightly bigger scale than you usually see. I do like the Japanese style gardens but sometimes I find they feel *too* manicured and sterile. I guess that's the whole point though.

 Some of the trees there were really interesting too. Really big ones, some had been nicely shaped over time, which I do find impressive, and I don't really understand the art behind it. Also one which just seemed like it wanted to die. That was my favourite.

Also was a greenhouse near the exit. Nothing to write home about, yet here I am.

After the park we went for lunch, to coco ichibanya. First time going, first time having Japanese curry here too. Really enjoyed it, I got level 2 spice mum got level 3. Both were a good level of spicy for us. Mine started off pretty warm and stayed at that level, mums seemed like a stronger flavour but not too spicy to start off with, but apparently it built over time. The pickled raddish thing that you get on the side is really good too. A bit sweet which cuts through nicely.


Did a bit of shopping at muji after that, as it's right next to the station. Was a bit of a shambles though because neither of us really needed to get anything, and I didn't see the clothing half of the shop until after we had paid for our stuff. It was a bit of fun anyway, got a few snacks and bits and bobs. Looked at the clothes afterwards too and to be honest I probably wouldn't have bought anything anyway.

Headed home, surprisingly tired given today was supposed to be lighter on the agenda. But it was already about 5 by the time we got home. Mum fell asleep for a bit before shower and dinner, which was sweet potato and random bits from the fridge and freezer. Nice to get some clean healthy nutrition in for a change.
