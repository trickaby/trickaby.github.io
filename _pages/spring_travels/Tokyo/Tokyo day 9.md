Returned to Ueno park today, having been a week ago. We've pretty much done everything we wanted to do in Tokyo now, which is nice. Obviously there's more to do here, but it's nice to be able to revisit places too.

Ueno was really nice, the cherry blossom is properly here now. It was super busy as a result, but still worth walking around. Last time we went, because it was a bit colder we spent a lot of time in the museum. This time though we walked around more, around a pond, just exploring more in general. Mum took some nice pictures too which will see the light of day at some point.

Next we went around some streets for shopping, we didn't spend long though as it tires both of us out. For lunch we went back to Ikebukuro, to the sushi place from a few days ago. We were a bit more adventurous this time, I even ordered something from the chef instead of just picking up what was on the conveyor belt. It was scary but I managed. I also drank lots of tea.

We had a half-hearted wander around the Ikebukuro area before jumping on the train home. Had the late afternoon to relax before a wander locally in the early evening. We found ourselves back at the park from the first day, which is really lovely, and also had some cherry trees which started to blossom. We might go back in a couple of days to see it in the light of day. Wandered around the library and got a sandwich from the cafe while we were there.

The Tokyo holiday feels like it's starting to wind down now, we've seen pretty much all we wanted to, enjoyed the transition into spring, done our shopping. Only a few days left until mum flies back, then I'll start figuring out what day-to-day ilfe is like here. I'm assuming just loads of famichiki and trying to find pull-up bars. 

I want to pick up a few more things when I get a bit more free time. I've got about 3 books to read, as well as a few games on the switch to play. I've also got a bit of a plan to buy a Japanese Nintendo DS, to play the Japanese versions of the old Pokémon games. I reckon they'll be at an ok language level by then, and I'll already have some context to help me along the way. I've also played them enough to probably be able to go through the story without being able to read much.
