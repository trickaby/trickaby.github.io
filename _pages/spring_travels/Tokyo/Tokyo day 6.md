Day after Fuji, no letup on the pace.

We started off in the imperial palace, with a bit of cloud that was threatening to turn into rain. The palace was very busy and a bit confusing with where to go, and a few times we had to leave the palace and come back in, having to go through another bag search. I think we didn't do the best route either, so didn't see all the good bits probably. Other than that it was alright, saw some nice trees and structures. Maybe a bit of a miss overall though as I think mum was expecting something a bit different.

Next we did a bunch of walking, first to Ginza, where we just walked through the main streets, didn't go  in anywhere. Grabbed some matcha to take back from a shop, but otherwise didn't shop much. It was on our way here that it started raining, so we weren't in the mood to wander around much. At least I wasn't.

Next we went to the Tsukiji fish market, and found a sushi place for lunch. It was pretty fancy and easily the most expensive meal we've had so far, but still fairly reasonable and the fish was really good. It tasted expensive, in a good way. Maybe we were just hungry.

Next was Hamarikyu gardens, a smaller garden compared to others we've been to, but actually surprisingly nice. It's by the harbourfront which is nice, makes it feel less busy and in the city. Also some nice ponds/rivers running through. We happened upon a tea house in the middle of it too, which came just at the right time in the day. We each got a matcha set, which was tea with a sweet. We shared the sweets. One was a little rice cake with red bean paste that was in the shape of a bird. The other was like a dorayaki, again with a red bean filling. That was probably nicer even though the bird looked more interesting. Tea was delicious of course. Hard to get wrong, tea.

Last up was a walk through the rain to teamLab borderless. It's one of the more touristy, mainstream places to go, but I think it's really good and lives up to the hype. Initially I was a bit underwhelmed having built it up in my mind, but after a while your brain adjusts to the pace of it all and relaxes into it. You're not supposed to go in trying to see everything and 'complete' it, more just live in the setting for a while. There were a few highlights thought, one of a tree with birds that started flying around turning into flowers. It's hard to explain but visually very impressive and emotive. Also liked the frogs and rabbits that were walking around the place. Best was probably the room with lilypad type fixtures. Again it's hard to explain and it's something better experienced for yourself, but the whole thing felt very immersive.

After that we went home, our feet pretty wet by then. Stopped at family mart for some comfort foods before showering and turning in.
