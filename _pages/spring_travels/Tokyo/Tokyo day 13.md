title: Day 13 - final day of spring travels!
---

Last day of spring travels was a bit sombre. Neither of us were really ready for it to end. For mum it meant having to go back to London, for me it meant losing a travel partner and having to settle in. Not really being on holiday anymore. I recognise I'm still basically on holiday compared to the average person, and in a privileged position to be able to do this. But still it felt different.

We tidied up ready to move out of our room, and headed to my new flat. We've both really enjoyed Tokyo, and Kami-Itabashi as a place to base ourselves. It was also a bit of a shock to the system having to lug our suitcases around the Tokyo metro, we've gotten used to travelling light recently.

Checked into my new flat in Kita-Shinjuku. It's a pretty nice area, maybe a little more rough and ready than other parts of Tokyo, but it's pretty central so I guess you sort of expect that. It's still a really good location, loads of stuff to do and shops nearby etc. One caveat is I'm right next to the train tracks, so I can hear trains going past all day. It's not too bad though, you sort of tune them out if you're doing anything important, and they don't affect my sleep.

We headed to Shinjuku for brunch, went to a sushi place where it arrives on a conveyer belt and you order on a tablet, all very modern. It was good but not as nice as some of the other sushi we've had in our time here. Still was a fun experience, and there was free tea so mum was happy.

Popped back 'home' to grab mum's luggage, then headed to the airport. The train there was very easy, as has been the case the whole time. Checked her luggage and walked around a little bit, before she went through security.

I took the monorail back home, which was a nice surprise as I didn't realise it existed. Like the bullet train, riding a monorail is really no different to a normal train. Still cool though.

Headed back home and got started on a few jobs, like shopping for essentials. Feel like I'm floating around in space a bit at the moment, so it will be good to start to settle into some routines to feel a bit more grounded.

And that's it for the spring adventure! Has been a really fun time, nothing really went wrong, saw pretty much everything we wanted to, me and mum didn't become enemies so all good. We did so much and had so many new experiences, I'm not sure my brain has really digested it all.

Writing this a few days later, and I've been sleeping a lot. Probably partly so my brain can rest a bit and catch up. Hopefully it solidifies some of the last month in my brain a bit before school starts. I don't want to forget what I've been up to so hopefully in the next few days I can have some down time and my brain will process everything. 

I'll try and keep up the blog during my time here, but frequency may drop a bit now that I'm starting Japanese school. We'll see.
