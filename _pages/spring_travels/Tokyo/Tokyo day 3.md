Bit of a lie in, neither of us awake until after 9. Had boiled eggs and toast for breakfast, which brought back Osaka memories. Food from supermarkets seems really good quality here. 

Headed to Ueno park late morning. Expected to see more cherry blossom here, but again it's still a little way off. The park itself was super busy but not in a suffocating way. I guess the walkways are wide enough to facilitate it, and no one is rushing around or clamouring towards a particular place.

We headed into the imperial museum, which is inside the park. In retrospect we probably could have saved it for a day with worse weather, but really enjoyed it nonetheless. The blades were probably my favourite part.

After the museum we walked back to the park exit, and noticed the cheery blossoms had opened up a bit more since the morning. It's been nice seeing it unfold over time.

We then walked towards Akihabara. There was the electric town there, which is all about anime and gaming and stuff, lot of young people about. We both enjoyed it to look at, although it was a lot to take in and a lot of the time I wasn't sure what I was looking at. Maybe one to return to another day.

Before that though, we stopped off at a tempura restaurant. It was just a random place that we wandered into, down a flight of stairs to the basement of a building. Shared a set meal and an order of gyoza. It was really good, one of my favourite meals so far. The tempura was really nicely fried, gyoza were crispy and juicy, and the selection of meat, seafood, eggs and vegetables were really tasty. What started off as a big plate of food was gone pretty quickly. My favourites were the chicken, egg, and lotus root.

After Asakusa we headed home. Still not feeling 100% and it seems like a good idea to save energy for Tuesday, when we're booked for a Fuji tour. Hopefully the weather holds, it's forecast to be the only clear day remaining for mum's stay in Tokyo.
