Yokohama isn't that far from Tokyo, and mercifully our new accommodation let us store our bags early. So we did our Japanese study in the morning, left at 9:30 and arrived in Tokyo an hour and a half later. We dumped our bags, walked around our local area, had a sandwich and cake at a local library cafe, then headed into town.

Where we're staying is fairly quiet and unassuming. Not super busy or built up. Seems pretty residential. I like it so far.

First we stopped off at Takadanobaba. It's where my language school is, so we walked over there to see where it is, before walking to my future long term Tokyo accommodation. Again, just to get a feel for the area and the walking route. To bo honest, there wasn't much to look at between the two places, so I may have to find a more interesting route, or see what the train/bus journey is like. I'm considering getting a bike to zip around town, but I'm not sure how much of a hassle it is to get set up with all that. 

We then carried on a bit further to Shinjuku centre. It's mad, just super busy and with bright lights. I don't think it's even the most lively part of Tokyo? Not sure. By this time we were pretty tired, so found the station entrance and headed back home.

Went to a tonkatsu place for dinner, near our room. It was actually really nice. I think it's a chain, but still, I got a pork and shrimp tonkatsu set meal, got mum the oyster, and total was about £12. Set meal just adds rice and miso soup, but for a filling and tasty meal I was impressed. Had some cabbage in there too for veg. Had some sauces and seasonings too to keep things interesting. Had togarashi and soy sauce too which made the rice more flavourful.

Grabbed some milk and back home. Pretty tired. But we're in Tokyo which is pretty darn cool. Looking forward to exploring the city more.

Also cherry blossom season has started! We weren't really targeting it, but since we're here it will be nice to see the city in bloom. We've already seen a tree here or there that's burst, and has been really nice. Planning to do things like Ueno park to get that full effect. Maybe go a few times to see how it develops over time.
