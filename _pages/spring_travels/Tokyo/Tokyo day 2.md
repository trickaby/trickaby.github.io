First day proper in Tokyo, was a bit of a slow start to be honest. After a long walk around town the previous day, we were pretty tired. I woke up with a bit of a sore throat, so we decided to take the day a bit slower. Did a shop and some laundry in the morning, ready to head out early afternoon.

I fell asleep for a few hours though, so we didn't leave home until about 3. Originally had planned to do Ueno park, Senso-ji and Tokyo skytree. Decided to ditch the park and head to Asakusa, to start off with Senso-ji. It was a really nice temple with busy market streets leading up to it. We got some pancake things which were really nice, and some takoyaki further up. 

We wandered around pretty aimlessly for a good while after that. We'd booked skytree for 7:30 so had some time to kill. Walked around Sumida park, which is near Sumida river. Was nice wallking around as the sun was setting. We've not really been out in the evenings that much, we tend to do more during the day then become tired and spend the evening in, writing blogs, studying Japanese, planning the next day etc. Also eating random konbini foods (mainly me).

Skytree was pretty cool, I'm still a bit baffled by the sheer size of Tokyo, my brain hasn't been able to wrap my head around it. Skytree is basically a big tower where you can see all aroud Tokyo from a height. It's crazy though because the city just keeps going on forever off to the horizon. When we got there it was night time, I'm not sure whether it would have been better to go during the day or night. But you get some really cool views of the twinkling city lights.

Cheery blossom season continues to creep on. It hasn't exploded yet like I thought it might. Looking forward to seeing how it unfolds though. Starting to get some nice pictures of trees in full bloom, but also seeing lots of rows of trees that are a day or two off. The trees that are in bloom get crowded by people trying to take photos, which annoys me a little to be honest. Few people seem to actually appreciate these things in person, it's all just experienced through their phones. Maybe I'm exaggerating a bit but the crowds are annoying. I'm also always aware of trying to get out of other people's photos, so I don't really take the time to enjoy the blossom myself.

Went back home after, pretty tired. Late night, not in bed until 11!
