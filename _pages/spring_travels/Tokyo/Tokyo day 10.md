Day trip to Kamakura!

Kamakura is south of Tokyo, near the coast. Train was best part of two hours door to door, but a pretty easy journey. We stopped off at a temple first, Meigetsu-in. It was nice, pretty peaceful and had a nicely kept garden. There were some hydrangeas that would probably look great once they've bloomed. Otherwise some pretty cool trees, nice gravel piles and other bits and bobs. To be honest I'm not sure how they actually make the gravel piles because they're very neat and tidy.

Next we walked into town. There's a bit of a shopping street but we accidentally walked past it and do be honest I wasn't that bothered about it anyway. We got to Kamakura station, where you can take a bus to the big Buddha.

We took a bus to the big Buddha, called Kotoku-in if we're being proper. Buddha was big, looked pretty nice. Loads of people trying to take a nice picture with it. We walked around the grounds a bit before heading back down to find lunch.

Lunch was this place that I randomly found on maps, was a bit more fancy style and we had to sit outside and wait for about half an hour, but I think the wait was worth it. We got the set lunch which was 7 small dishes and rice served in a clay pot, and you choose what flavour to go in the rice. Mum got whitebait, I had pork and mustard greens. Both were nice. It was fun trying all the small dishes too, although to be honest we had no idea what a lot of it was.

We carried on walking down to the coast, where we walked along the beach, which was of the sandy variety. I haven't properly been to the beach in probably years, so it was a bit of a treat. Weather was good too, sunny and temperatures maybe just about touching 20. Walked along the water's edge. The tide was going out. Water was not that cold. There were a decent number of surfers but no one just swimming. It made me wonder about Japanese beach culture, because it seems somewhat different to back home.

Started to cool down as the afternoon went on, so we cut inside back towards Kamakura station to get our train home. We scouted out for ice cream on the way back though, because it felt like the right thing to do. We ended up right next to the station getting some tea flavoured ice creams, one matcha one hojicha. Both were good but I really liked the hojicha, don't think I've tried that yet.

Got the train home, which was very easy. Then back home by 6 by which time we were pretty worn out. Luckily I seem to have avoided bringing too much sand back with me.
