Last full day before mum flies back. We headed back to the imperial palace since we didn't think we did a god job of seeing it the first time around. We were right.

Basically the first time we got there, we went down a main street which is where the actual palace is, but it's down a road so you can't get too close which is fair enough. The rest of it was just one path though, and very crowded. Then you go out and back in again, going through bag search a second time. So we went in, turned left, left again, then ended up at the exit, having missed the juicy middle that was worth seeing. This time around, we went straight to the juice, and it was really nice.

It's not got loads of history or buildings to be honest, not loads going on, but there's enough to keep you interested. The gardens are also pretty nice, with a few cherry trees as well as plum and others. There was a 10-20 second moment where a gust of wind came and blew loads of blossom off the cherry trees. It floated in the air a while before falling like snow. After that it was back to normal as if nothing happened. We're getting to the stage now where some of the cherry blossom is finishing, and others are coming in. It's cool because some of the petals are on the ground now, again it's like snowfall.

The rain started to come down as we were finishing up, so we headed back to Ikebukuro, walked around some shops. Didn't really find much, I guess neither of us were in the mood really. But it is funny how here you can just find yourself in a 10+ story shopping centre straight out of the station.

Headed back home after this, to catch up on Japanese practice and blog writing.

Went out before it got dark, again to the local park one last time. Got a tea and cake again from the cafe. Really enjoyed staying in Kami-Itabashi.

Headed back home to relax and start packing for moving day tomorrow.
