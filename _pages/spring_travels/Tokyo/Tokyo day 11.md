We went back to Shinjiku park, having liked it so much the first time. Weather was really nice again, probably the first properly warm day where we should have worn less. Very good to walk around and see parts of the park that we'd not seen before. Got nice and tired ahead of lunch.

It's a park that's not too far from my accommodation for the next few months. I'm in Kita-Shinjuku, so it's probably a long walk or a short train ride away. They have an annual pass for not too much money, so I may decide to grab one if I think it's worth it. Would be a good place to go to think.

Hopefully it would be less crowded outside of cherry blossom season too. There is still a bit too much of people going around clogging up the nice parts trying to take photos. We did find some more peaceful parts along the outskirts, like the mother and child garden which is a bit more of a sustainable managed garden, so helping promote biodiversity and all that good stuff. One thing I was learning about recently just through internets, is how these days there aren't as many bugs, and also there are fewer insect polinated plants these days, which is part of why people suffer with hay fever so much. Because there's more stuff that's wind pollinated, which is where all the pollen is flying around in the air. To be honest I haven't checked out if any of this is actually true or just internet nonsense, but hopefully one day I will. Or you can and let me know. That would be great, thanks.

Lunch we found a place nearby which was another Japanese set meal. This time I got tempura fish and vegetables. It came with 2 sides, miso and rice. All very yummy. We also got a small sashimi side to share which was good too, bit more fresh and tangy tasting. The restaurant seemed to be really high on onions, they were everywhere. In the soup, the sashimi, my tempura. Not complaining but I found it interesting. 

Headed back home for a bit, we'd planned to go to the local park again later. Getting to the point now where we're getting tired a bit faster, having had so many days without a day off. I think when mum goes back I'm going to start exercising proper again, but also have at least one day where I just lie in bed.

We did manage to get out later in the day, which sometimes doesn't happen because we're tired and comfortable at home. But we went to a local bento place, got some bento, then went to the park to eat bento. I had the mixed fry, mum seafood. Both were good but pretty similar. The menu was big and I struggled to know what would be best to get. It also came with a little bit of spaghetti on the side, seasoned with some mystery flavour.

Park was nice, it's a super wholesome place to hang out. Loads of kids running around playing, big trees in various stages of their seasonal changes. The library is attached too, so people come in and out. Everyone rides bicycles to get there. It was a socialist's paradise. It's the sort of place that makes me feel like Japan would be a super place to grow up.

We went home and watched Oppenheimer. We've both seen it before but I wanted to revisit it a bit having been to Hiroshima, see if it changed the way I viewed it. It didn't really, but I feel like I'm a little better informed now. Other than that it's just a really good film.

Had the usual milk and biscuits before bed. Haven't really talked about this much but biscuits back home are way better. Probably needs a whole dedicated post to be honest.
