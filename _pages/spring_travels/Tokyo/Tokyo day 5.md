Fuji day!

Woke at 6, left at 6:30, to meet in Shinjuku at 7:15.

I didn't know this but Shinjuku station is the busiest in the world. I'm not at all surprised by this, it's manic. And huge. As you'd expect though, there's a calm to the chaos. Everyone is just going to their own place, no pushing or rushing really. There's just loads of people, loads of trains and lines, but it's all very orderly.

That being said, I don't regret that my commute will be walkable.

Took a coach to Kawaguchi lake, where you get a good view of Fuji. Skies were completely clear in the morning so we got some great pictures. This particular area has benefitted from tourism to see Fuji. Other places we saw later in the day had had a population drain to the city, which is a tale you seem to hear a lot of these days, in all countries. 

Next we went to Ōishi park. There they had some nice flower beds and some fields. But the star of the show was the ice cream. Me and mum shared a blueberry and a grape flavour. Surprisingly the grape flavour was the standout.

Next we did a noodle making experience thing, mums noodles were really good and mine were fairly bad, as you'd expect. Next we had to eat what we'd made, in Hōtō, which is a chicken and vegetable miso soup. Really flavourful and had seasonal veg. I remember tasting leek, is that in season?

Clouds started to come over in the afternoon, but only a bit. We went to the 8 ponds that are formed from water from Fuji. The water was super clear but by that time of day it was super busy, so hard to take things in. 

Last was the pagoda, I forget the name. We walk up 398 steps to the top, it wasn't that hard. View from the top was good, with Fuji slightly obscured by clouds. 

We took the coach back to Tokyo after that. It's about an hour and a half which gave me time to study Japanese and catch up on blog writing. I did my Japanese and watched YouTube videos.

This blog is being written when I should be having a shower and getting ready for bed. I suspect I'll have a shower when I should be asleep, and just sleep until I'm awake
