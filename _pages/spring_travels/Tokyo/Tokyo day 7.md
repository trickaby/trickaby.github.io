The plan today was to go to Shinjuku and tackle the big park there that we didn't get round to on our first day. We stopped off for a pancake lunch at a place in the shopping centre by the station. we got pancakes. It was way too much food and I was very full by the end. Weird how pancakes can be so light but so filling. Probably also because we'd had a lazy morning so the metabolism probably wasn't firing on all cylinders. Also very sweet which even I can get too much of eventually. Still really enjoyed it though, we got one strawberry flavour which was the really fluffy style of pancake, and one apple which was all toffee-cinnamon-y.

Walked around the shops of the department store, not really with an eye to buy anything, more just curious to see what they had. When I went to department stores in Korea, they seemed a bit too full of western expensive brands, at the same price as back home, so I wasn't really interested much. I did get a coat and jumper that were very snazzy and expensive, mind. They were a Korean brand though which is what made me decide to get them, because I wouldn't find the same back home. Here though there seemed to be more unfamiliar brands and styles, which I found more interesting. Not interesting enough to shops with any great purpose, but interesting. Anyway, tangent over.

We headed outside to the sound and sight of pouring rain, which we'd come prepared for. Perhaps we weren't mentally prepared though, because we both took one look and decided to change course. There was a uniqlo nearby that did tax free shopping. It was on our itinerary at some point, so we headed there instead. Bought a bunch of basics much cheaper that it would have been back home. Was surprised to find a pair of trousers that fit me alright too. Usually all the sizes are too short in the leg, but these were ok. I think the recent trend towards oversizing is helping me a bit in this regard.

Headed back home after this, arms heavy with the weight of shopping bags. The journey back from Shinjuku is pretty easy, 2 trains one stop each. If you catch the semi-fast train, that is.

Relaxed at home for a bit in the late afternoon, still full from breakfast. We were both pretty tired from the previous day, being in the rain that long feels like it wears you out more for some reason. 

Went to eat locally at an izakaya for dinner, where we got some tempura and rice and fish. I don't know if there's a name for a big bowl of rice with sashimi style fish on top, but that's what it was. Came with a pleasant array of vegetable sides and pickles, as well as the usual miso soup. I do really enjoy miso, I must admit.

So overall not a very eventful day, but it was one that felt a bit more 'real'. Walking around, dodging the weather, doing some mundane shopping, popping into a local eatery. Nothing too crazy or out of the ordinary. Apart from the pancakes I suppose, they were a bit crazy.
