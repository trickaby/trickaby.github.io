Bit more of a relaxed day, we went to Shibuya, Yoyogi park and Meji Jingu shrine. They were all pretty good, nothing spectacular really. Probably at the point now where the shrines are starting to blend together. The park was really good though, lovely trees and not just cherry ones. 

After walking around we went to find late lunch. Walked around the Harajuku area for a bit but it was crazy busy and didn't really see anything for us. Instead we headed to Ikebukuro, where there was a conveyer belt sushi restaurant that had been recommended to me, so we went. If you don't take mum for sushi every few days she'll start glitching out so I thought it was overdue. Sushi was really good, the guy sitting next to me showed us how to order, which was nice of him. I spent the whole time trying to act like I knew what I was doing, but when he asked and I said no, he was very eager to help, and I started to enjoy myself a lot more. Definitely a lesson in there. A fun moment was when the chefs had a change of shift. Because there's a conveyor belt, the chefs, in the middle, have to crawl out from underneath, and the new ones crawl in. There was something contradictory about these master sushi chefs, who have worked tirelessly to prepare such sophisticated food, being reduced to crawling through a hatch to leave their post.

The sushi in general was great. We tried all sorts, including a natto one, which surprisingly mum really liked. I didn't, but maybe that's because I knew what was in it. I'm still a bit traumatised from Hokkaido.

Evening relaxed, had to get an early night to be on time for Fuji in the morning
