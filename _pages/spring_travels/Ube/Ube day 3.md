Decided to head out for a walk today, so we went to Tokiwa park. Didn't really know what to expect of it, but it was the nearest blob of green on google maps so we went there.

Took about 15 minutes to reach the start of the park, which had a big paved path going through some dense woods. Eventually it opened up a bit, to a viewpoint overlooking a big pond.

The park loops round the whole pond, with a few attractions on the far side from where we were. We wondered round, looked at some of the sculptures in the sculpture park, saw a shrine that was further into the pond via a bridge, saw a pelican enclosure, continued round until we were back where we started.

This was the best day so far in terms of weather. Mid teens and very sunny. The park was surprisingly good too, I think it's pretty famous locally so it was lucky we decided to head there of all places. I guess there's not actually that much else to see where we were, so maybe it's not so much luck as just the forced path.

For lunch we went to the local cafe. It was about 40 minutes away from the park. We had omurice, which was good and worth trying, but the portions were huge. Still managed to finish everything but I wasn't hungry again until tomorrow.

Walked back home after that, another 35 minutes. Everything in this town is far away, not really a walker friendly place for that reason. It's like going to the countryside in England where everything is a short drive away because it's so sparsely built up. 

Onsen this time was a bit later, around sunset. Was a really nice time to go, although I think I got bitten a few times as a result.

We got more tea this evening, I popped to the shops for some milk too. Spoke with the owner of the onsen, who offered to drive us to the station in the morning. Ube has been a very friendly place.
