Took a total of 4 trains to get to Onomichi, but honestly it was a pretty easy journey. Travelling in general is tiring just because you've got to pay attention and lug suitcases around. 

2 short trains to get to Shin-Osaka, then bullet train to Fukuyama, then a local train to Onomichi. Only hiccup was missing a train at Fukuyama, and having to wait 45 minutes for the next one. But that's ok we've done pretty well so far overall.

Buying the ticket was a little tricky at Shin-Osaka because I couldn't find Onomichi as a destination, so instead we went to the counter and asked someone to buy it for us, which was a lot more straightforward.

We got ekiben to eat but that was a bit unnecessary considering our train was 11:02-12:04. Oh well, at least dinner is sorted.

The place we're staying at is pretty cool. It's a guesthouse that used to be student housing in the Showa era. We have a room to ourselves but shower, kitchen, bathroom are shared. Also we have the futon and tatami setup instead of beds. It's pretty cool but I hope mum doesn't mind too much.

Did a quick shop for food, needed to stock up as I'm doing the Shiminami Kaido tomorrow. Will stay the night in Imabari then return the following day.
