Second week in Noboribetsu was largely uneventful. I eventually got over my jetlag, but was still waking up fairly early and having to take the odd nap during the day. Got out for gyoza and karage but whenever I went to the ramen place it was full. Not too fussed, I'm sure I'll get lots of ramen opportunities in the future.

A few walking paths were closed for the winter, so I didn't get around much, mainly just relaxing and enjoying the onsen. I tended to go in the evening, did go in the morning once but with breakfast the timings are a bit tight which makes it less relaxing. 

I also started doing the sauna/cold plunge routine too, where I'd cycle between the two. By the end of my stay I was able to stay in the cold for a good 5-10 minutes. The trick is to not move at all.

One thing I found annoying was whenever I got out of a hot pool, I'd get dizzy and have to just stay still for a while. This happens to me quite often anyways but you've just got to be a bit careful not to pass out.

Train back to the airport was all good, no issues. Everything has run very smoothly so far.

Probably the coolest parts have been when it's snowed while I'm in the onsen. It's just a really unique atmosphere with the hot and cold meeting.

Hokkaido itself seems very remote which I found a bit daunting. Trains and buses were infrequent, and there wasn't much to accommodate non Japanese speakers. Travelling back to the airport and seeing the surroundings, it seems very rural, not much going on except huge snowy mountains and farmland. The onsen itself was tiny, and you could only really get there from the station via taxi or an infrequent bus. 

I think if I came to Hokkaido again I'd stay in Sapporo, to see more of what the area is like, maybe be a bit more active and travel around. This time I was being careful not to do too much, cos the game plan was relaxation. Looking forward to spring travels now
